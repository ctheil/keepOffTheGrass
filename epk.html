BIO

Born with the Rocky Mountains as the backdrop, Keep Off the Grass is an indie/rock band with midwest emo influences. Forming in 2018, the college roommates-turned-bandmates were playing the Denver scene and enjoying their time in pre-COVID existence. With the onset of quarantine, the group decided to bunker down and hyper-analyze their music. They coined their sound as “mountain emo.” With the musical timbre of bands such as American Football and Manchester Orchestra, and the lyrical ambiance of artists like Bright Eyes, Keep Off the Grass is poised to entice listeners on the entire alternative spectrum

What Is Mountain Emo?

Mountain-Emo is a unique blend of indie/rock, folk/rock, math rock, and midwest-emo. It carries the vocal tonality of artists like Bright Eyes and the guitar style of Johnny Marr (The Smiths)… the groove of an early Radiohead song and the instrumental tone of a Sunny Day Real Estate song. Its niche, yet broad enough to appeal to listeners all over the board.

[Wemple’s] vocal inflections are subtle and evocative, and serve as his primary weapon as he unveils doubts and insecurities about himself and the world around him.

— MITCH MOSK (ATWOOD MAGAZINE)
